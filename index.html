Ayurveda

Ayurveda is the ancient knowledge cultivated by the sages of India.
It has been developed from various treatises and manuscripts and has made its way to becoming one of the most ancient but modern health care techniques.
‘Ayur’ means life and ‘Veda’ means Knowledge.
Thus, the term ‘Ayurveda’ means ‘Knowledge of Life’.
The theories and notions of Ayurveda have been practiced since the past 5000 years. It recommends methods for appropriate living and longevity.
It includes instructions to maintain good health as well as dealing with illness through Yog-abhyas, herbal medicines, dietary discpline and lifestyle changes.

Panchakarma

Ayurveda believes that all diseases are caused as a result of an imbalance between the body constituents. This imbalance leads to formation of various toxins.
To Eliminate the toxins (shodhana Chikitsa) by way of the Panchakarma’s.
This involves the main five procedures hence also called as the Panchakarma's. (Pancha=five, karma=procedures). Each of these Panchakarma’s are aimed at eliminating the aggravation in one or more of the body constituents. The classical Panchakarma's are

Vamana

It is a process in which waste products (kaphaj doshas) are eliminated through the upper channels i.e. through the mouth by the process of Vomiting. Read More..

Virechan

Virechana therapy is a type of Panchakarma treatment. Here, person is made to undergo controlled purgation for a specific amount of time. It is designed to flush out body toxins. Read More..

Nasyam

Nasyam refers to a procedure where the medicated drugs or oils are administrated through the nostrils. Nasal medication is especially described for the treatment of diseases of the parts above the shoulders. Read More..

Basti

Basti refers to administration of enema with medicated oil and herbal decoctions . It is one of the five classical Detoxification programme (Panchakarma). Read More..

Raktamoskhan

Though it sounds very primitive way of treating, it is a very helpful process. Raktamokshana means removing vitiated blood from the body. It is a localized treatment . Generally not More than 10-40 ml blood is removed at a time Read More..

Treatments

Snehana

Snehan or Abhyanga, is one of the most important dincharyas (daily regimen) mentioned in the classics for achieving good health and longevity. Read More..

Swedana

Fomentation is carried out using the steam generated by boiling herbal decoctions.The herbs for the decoction are selected according to the patient’s constitution and condition.It helps in breaking the adhesions between the toxins and the body tissues. Read More..

Shirodhara

Shirodhara massage is a classic Ayurvedic therapy, which includes pouring of warm herbal oil on the forehead in a continuous stream. Ayurvedic Shirodhara therapy is very effective in promoting sound sleep and reducing stress. Read More..

Netratarpan

In Netra Tarpanam, specially prepared luke warm ghee or oil is retained over the eye socket inside a well created using black gram paste or whole wheat flour. Read More..

Janu Basti

Janu Sandhi means the knee joint. Janu Basti involves pouring of specially prepared warm herbal oil over the knee. Read More..

Hridaya Basti

Hridaya Basti involves pouring of specially prepared warm herbal oil over the chest to relieve heart problems.

Manya Basti

Manya Basti involves pouring of specially prepared warm herbal oil over the back of the neck to relieve neck pain problems. Read More..

Kati Basti

Kati Basti specially prepared warm herbal oil is poured over the lower back and retained inside a well created using black gram paste or whole wheat flour. Read More..

Saundarya Chikitsa

Soundarya is a classical combination of a full body Ayurvedic Abhyanga massage that is followed by application of Ayurvedic mask containing paste of skin-nourishing fruit supplements. Read More..
